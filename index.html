Overview

UAVLight is designed to study how outdoor 3D reconstruction methods behave under significant illumination changes in real UAV captures, moving beyond the standard single-time acquisition setting.

Existing outdoor reconstruction benchmarks often assume relatively stable appearance and limited illumination variation. UAVLight instead focuses on large-scale real-world scenes captured across multiple time slots, providing a dedicated benchmark for evaluating reconstruction robustness under substantial sunlight and shadow changes.

Illumination Variation & Protocol

The core challenge in UAVLight is that similar viewpoints can exhibit drastically different appearance due to time-of-day dependent lighting. Our protocol explicitly isolates this challenge for quantitative evaluation.

Paired cross-light evaluation protocol

Lighting is estimated from one subset of views and applied to another subset captured at the same time slot, enabling a controlled and challenging cross-light benchmark.

Benchmark Focus

UAVLight is not only a dataset release, but also a benchmark designed to test whether reconstruction methods remain stable under large appearance shifts caused by changing sunlight and shadows.

  • Real outdoor UAV scenes captured across multiple time slots.
  • Cross-light protocol to stress illumination robustness.
  • Evaluation using PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS.
  • Suitable for both NeRF-style and 3DGS-style methods.
